MEMS microphone

The MEMS microphone was invented in 1983 but didnâ€™t become popular until the 2000s. They are used to detect finger movements in touchscreens. They are used in many devices including earring aids, cell phones, ipads, and also smart home devices. How they work is that when a sound waves hits the membrane of the MEMS microphone it changes the capacitance and that makes an electronic signal. One benefit from the MEMS microphone is that since it is very small it can clear out background noise and make out clear voices. Another benefit is that they are small and low power.
